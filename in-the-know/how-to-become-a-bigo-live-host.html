How to Become a Paid BIGO Live Host in 2026

FZ Agency◆ July 2, 2026◆ 5 min read

If you love being on camera, meeting people, and the idea of getting paid to do it, becoming a BIGO Live host might be the side hustle — or full-time move — you've been looking for. This guide walks you through exactly how it works, what you need, and how the money actually adds up.

What is a BIGO Live host?

BIGO Live is a global live-streaming app where creators go live, entertain an audience, and earn through virtual gifts that viewers send during their streams. A "host" is simply a creator who streams regularly. When you join an agency — like FZ Agency — you get support, coaching, and access to events and bonuses that solo streamers don't have.

What you need to get started

The barrier to entry is low. You really only need three things:

  • A smartphone with a decent camera and a stable internet connection.
  • Good lighting (a simple ring light goes a long way) and a spot where you can talk freely.
  • Energy and consistency — the hosts who win are the ones who show up.

You must also be 18 or older to become a paid host.

Step 1: Download BIGO Live and create your profile

Grab the BIGO Live app from the App Store or Google Play and sign up. Add a clear profile photo, a short bio, and get comfortable with the app before your first stream. First impressions matter, so make your profile feel welcoming.

Step 2: Join an agency

You can stream on your own, but joining an agency is how most successful hosts grow faster. With FZ Agency you get orientation, mentorship from hosts who've done it, and a whole family cheering you on. There are two easy ways to apply:

  • On our site: fill out the quick application form and we'll reach out to set up your host status.
  • In the app: tap Me → Creator Center → Become a Broadcaster, then search FashZip and tap Apply.

Step 3: Learn how the money works

Once you're in, orientation covers the part everyone wants to know about: earnings. On BIGO Live, your income is driven by three things working together:

  • Gifts (Beans): viewers send virtual gifts during your stream, which convert into earnings.
  • Hours streamed: consistent live hours unlock higher tiers and rewards.
  • Incentives: agency and platform bonuses reward you for hitting goals.

Top hosts in our family have earned up to $64,000 in a month. That's the ceiling, not the average — real earnings depend on your hours, your audience, and the effort you put in. But the opportunity is genuinely there, and we help you chase it.

Step 4: Build your audience and have fun

The hosts who grow fastest treat it like a real show: a consistent schedule, a personality people come back for, and interaction with everyone who joins. Lean on your agency family, join PKs and events, and keep improving. The community is half the fun — and half the income.
